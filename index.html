About

I deviated away from my undergraduate studies in Fine Art ('05 - '09) to practise in IT. I had already used computers and technology for almost two decades. It was due to my flare with audio and video editing (in my course), toying with the family router, printers, MS-DOS family pc and much, much more that I took an interest in the Masters conversion course at University College Dublin ('12 - '13).

Introduced to a foundation of Web Development, Relational Database Design and various programming language modules I passed through the postgraduate world unscathed. My path, after working in an internship shortly after my studies and Realex Payments for a brief 11 months, was slightly bumpy when I was without work for a total of 8 months (3 months before and 5 months after starting at Realex). Nonetheless, I continued to work ahead to seek my next adventure and shortly after being accepted on to a Springboard programme (WebElevate 4.0) I was given another chance where I began to work in one of the top IT Solutions companies (with over 80 customers) for Credit Unions in Ireland. The company has customers based in Northern Ireland, Republic of Ireland and Scotland.

Currently I am in a new role based within Shared Services for Wellington IT Solutions. The role involves continuous proactive monitoring of various services. These services include the processing of electronic payments, Irish Credit Bureau checks and extracts, ATM transmission files, third party files, updating the Financial Sanctions List and DD originator details, the creation and tailoring of Nagios plugins, validation of the SEPA Routing Table, checking on the status of Credit Union sites (connectivity, load, processes, alerts and free space) and more.
